# What Counts as a Default

A loan is flagged as "defaulted" when three or more scheduled daily debits are skipped. This threshold is applied cumulatively over the life of the loan, not on consecutive days alone.

The reason for the three-skip threshold is that occasional skips are an expected part of daily earning — one bad day should never mean default. Three or more signals a pattern rather than a one-off, and is the point at which our risk scoring treats the borrower as struggling rather than bumping.

A defaulted loan does not automatically close. It remains active and daily debits keep attempting. The default flag is a signal to the user, to the risk engine, and to the assistant — not an administrative closing of the loan.
