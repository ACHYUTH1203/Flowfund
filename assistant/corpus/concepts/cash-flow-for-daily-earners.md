# Cash Flow for Daily Earners

A kirana shop owner's earnings are not a smooth Rs 1,000-per-day stream. They are a spiky, weekly-cyclic series that averages out to Rs 1,000 only when you look at long windows.

Typical patterns include weekends earning 1.5 times weekday averages (residential neighbourhoods), rainy days earning half as much, month-start peaks when salaried customers have cash, and month-end troughs when they do not. Festival weeks can spike or crash depending on inventory investment and local demand. Illness, closure for a family event, or a week of monsoon flooding can turn into multi-day income droughts.

For daily earners, cash flow — the actual rupees-in-hand today — matters more than profit on paper. A shop can be profitable over a month and still miss a loan payment on Tuesday. This is why FlowFund asks for average daily income, computes daily repayment from it, and stress-tests the resulting schedule against realistic variability (the Monte Carlo simulation) before recommending a loan as sustainable.
