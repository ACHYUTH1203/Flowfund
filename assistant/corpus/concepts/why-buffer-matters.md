# Why the Wallet Buffer Matters

Daily earnings are not smooth. A kirana store's income varies by day of the week, season, weather, and a hundred small causes. Some days earn 40 percent less than average; some earn 40 percent more. Monthly averages hide this spiky reality.

A wallet buffer is the shock absorber that separates normal noise from loan default. Without a buffer, a single 40 percent-down day directly causes a skipped debit. With a three-day buffer, that same down day is absorbed: the wallet has enough reserve to cover the debit, and the next normal day's income replenishes the buffer.

Concretely: if daily repayment is Rs 200 and the buffer is Rs 600, the borrower survives an unbroken streak of three zero-income days without a skip. For most small businesses, that is enough cushion for ordinary weekly variability. Extreme events (illness, festival cash crunch, monsoon flooding) still require planning, but the daily noise is handled.
