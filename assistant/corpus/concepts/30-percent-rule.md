# The 30 Percent Rule

ClickPe Lite caps the recommended daily repayment at 30 percent of the borrower's average daily income. This is the single most important affordability guardrail in the product.

The logic is simple. A small business owner also has daily running expenses (rent, inventory, food, other commitments), and needs a margin of safety against bad days. If we let loan repayment take more than 30 percent of daily income, the borrower has too little left to absorb the normal ups and downs of running a shop.

When the calculated daily repayment (based on loan amount, duration, and interest) exceeds 30 percent of daily income, the loan is flagged as "unsustainable". The recommended daily repayment is then capped at the 30 percent figure, which means the loan will take longer to repay than the originally requested duration. The borrower can choose to accept this longer effective duration, or revise the loan to a smaller amount or longer tenor.
