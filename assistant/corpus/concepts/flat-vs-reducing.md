# Flat Interest vs Reducing Balance Interest

ClickPe Lite uses flat interest. This means interest is calculated once, upfront, on the full principal amount — not on the reducing outstanding balance as the loan is repaid.

For a loan of Rs 10,000 at 10 percent flat interest, the total interest is Rs 1,000, making the total payable Rs 11,000 regardless of how fast it is repaid.

Reducing balance interest, used in most bank home loans, instead recalculates interest on the outstanding balance each period. The same nominal rate on a reducing balance loan costs less in absolute rupees because the interest base shrinks as the borrower pays down principal.

Flat interest is common for short-duration micro-loans because it is simpler to compute and present. Borrowers should be aware that a flat rate is not directly comparable to a reducing-balance rate — a 10 percent flat loan over 100 days costs more in effective terms than a 10 percent reducing-balance loan over the same period.
