# FAQ: Why is my risk score HIGH?

Question: My loan's risk score is HIGH. Why?

Answer: The risk engine looks at three independent signals. Any single HIGH-severity signal is enough to make the overall score HIGH.

1. Income sustainability. If your loan's base daily repayment exceeds 30 percent of your average daily income, the loan is structurally too large for your cash flow. This is the strongest and most common HIGH trigger.

2. Wallet buffer. If your current wallet balance divided by the daily repayment gives fewer than one full day of coverage, you cannot meet even tomorrow's debit. This fires HIGH immediately.

3. Skip rate on actual debits. If you have had at least three debit attempts and 20 percent or more were skipped, the engine treats this as evidence of repeated stress — which fires HIGH.

The "reasons" field on your risk score lists exactly which of these fired and why. The recommended action — typically to top up your wallet to a three-day buffer, or to review the loan's terms — is derived from whichever signal is pushing you up.

A HIGH flag is informational, not punitive. The loan continues to run. The flag exists so you can take action before things get worse.
