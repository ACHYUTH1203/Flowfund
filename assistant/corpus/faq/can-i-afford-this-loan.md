# FAQ: Can I afford this loan?

Question: How do I know whether I can afford a particular loan?

Answer: Three checks, in order.

First, the 30 percent rule. The loan's daily repayment should be at most 30 percent of your average daily income. If it is higher, the loan is flagged "unsustainable" and the recommended daily repayment will be capped — which means the loan takes longer to repay than advertised.

Second, the buffer check. After the loan is created, you should be able to maintain at least three times the daily repayment in your wallet at all times. If keeping that buffer feels like a stretch given your other daily expenses, the loan is too large for you right now.

Third, the stress test. Run a Monte Carlo simulation on the proposed loan. If the default probability with a realistic income pattern is above, say, 10 percent, the loan is risky for you even if the math on averages looks fine. A loan that defaults in one of ten realistic futures is a loan you should not be taking.

This is a recommendation based on your specific data, not professional financial advice.
