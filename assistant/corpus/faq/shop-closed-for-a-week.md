# FAQ: My shop will be closed for a week. What do I do?

Question: I need to close my shop for a week (illness, family event, travel). How do I handle loan payments during that time?

Answer: Plan for it before the closure begins. Your options, from best to worst.

Best: pre-fund your wallet with enough to cover every daily debit during the closure, plus the three-day buffer you would normally keep. For a seven-day closure at Rs 200 daily repayment, that is Rs 1,400 for the closure plus Rs 600 buffer, so top up to at least Rs 2,000 before the shop closes. All debits run normally; nothing is skipped.

Acceptable: pre-fund partially. If you can only cover half the closure, the rest of the days will skip. Three or more skipped days would trigger the default flag. Try to cover at least the first few days and top up the wallet mid-closure if you can.

Worst: do nothing and assume the loan pauses. The loan does not pause on its own. Debits continue to attempt every day. Skipped days accumulate. You may come back to a defaulted loan and a HIGH risk flag.

If the closure is unexpected and you are already in it, top up the wallet as soon as you have any cash, even partially. Every rupee in the wallet is a debit that runs instead of skipping.
