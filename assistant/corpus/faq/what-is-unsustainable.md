# FAQ: What does "unsustainable" mean on my loan?

Question: My loan shows "is_sustainable: false" or "unsustainable". What does that mean?

Answer: A loan is flagged unsustainable when the base daily repayment — that is, total payable divided by duration — is more than 30 percent of your average daily income.

This does not mean the loan is denied. It means the product is telling you that repaying this loan at the pace the loan contract implies would consume too much of your daily cash flow to be safe. To protect you, the system caps the recommended daily repayment at 30 percent of income and uses that capped amount for actual daily debits.

The practical consequence: the loan will take longer than the originally requested duration to fully repay. If you wanted a 100-day loan but your income can only support a 30 percent cap, the effective duration may stretch to 150 days or more. The loan is still usable — just think of the original duration as a target rather than a guarantee.

If you want a truly 100-day loan, either reduce the amount or increase your effective daily income (lower expenses, add a second income source) before applying.
