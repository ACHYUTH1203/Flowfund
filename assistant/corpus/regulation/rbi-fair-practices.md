# RBI Fair Practices Code for Lenders

The Reserve Bank of India publishes a Fair Practices Code that all regulated lenders, including NBFCs and micro-finance institutions, are expected to follow. It is not a single document but a set of principles that appear across RBI master directions.

Key principles include:
- Transparency: all terms, interest rates, fees, and charges must be communicated to the borrower clearly and in a language the borrower understands, before the loan is sanctioned.
- No misleading advertising: lending ads must not overstate benefits or understate costs.
- No harassment during collection: field recovery must follow hours of reasonable decency and cannot involve threats, public shaming, or contact with unrelated persons.
- Grievance redressal: every lender must provide a clear grievance officer and escalation path.
- Cooling-off period for certain product categories, giving the borrower a short window to exit without penalty.

Educational summary — not legal advice. For authoritative current rules, consult RBI's official notifications and the Fair Practices Code documentation.
