# Rights of Borrowers

Under RBI guidelines and broader consumer protection law, borrowers in India have a set of rights that apply regardless of the lender:

- The right to clear, complete information about the loan before it is disbursed — including interest, fees, schedule, and consequences of default.
- The right to a written grievance redressal mechanism and a named officer to escalate to if something goes wrong.
- The right to privacy: personal and financial data collected for the loan cannot be shared or used for other purposes without consent. The Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 strengthens these controls.
- The right to be free of harassment during collection: field agents cannot threaten, publicly shame, or contact unrelated persons.
- The right to understand the loan in a language the borrower prefers, where reasonably possible.

A borrower who believes a lender has violated these rights can file a complaint with the lender's grievance officer, and escalate to the RBI Ombudsman if unresolved within the stipulated timeframe.

Educational summary — not legal advice. Consult RBI and the Ministry of Finance for current definitive rules.
