# Interest Rate Disclosure Rules

Indian lending regulation requires that borrowers be shown the effective cost of credit, not just a nominal rate. For short-duration daily-repayment products, this matters because a low-looking flat rate can translate into a high annualised effective rate.

Key disclosure expectations:
- The nominal interest rate (for example, 10 percent flat) must be stated.
- The total amount repayable over the life of the loan must be shown clearly, so the borrower sees the rupees of interest being charged, not only the rate.
- Any processing fees, service charges, or add-on costs must be itemised; they cannot be hidden inside the headline rate.
- For products that convert a rate across tenors (daily, monthly, annual), the lender should make clear which is being used.
